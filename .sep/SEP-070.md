# SEP-070: Bridge market and regulatory data into deep research synthesis

**Status:** Open
**Created:** 2026-03-28
**Project:** zorora

## Summary
Inject relevant time-series data from market_data.db and regulatory_data.db (EIA, Eskom, SAPP, FRED, World Bank indicators, Ember) into the deep research synthesis prompt when query topics match available series, not just for diligence queries

## Motivation
Zorora has 64+ time series across 5 providers (FRED, yfinance, World Bank, SAPP, Eskom) plus regulatory data (EIA, OpenEI, RPS, NERSA). This data surfaces through Global View and Regulatory tabs but is invisible to deep research synthesis for non-diligence queries. Published research articles rely heavily on quantitative market data — the data exists in Zorora but never reaches the reasoning model during regular research.

## Proposed Change
Add a market context injection step to the deep research pipeline that runs for all research queries (not just diligence). Match query keywords against SERIES_CATALOG descriptions and regulatory data topics. For matching series, fetch recent observations and format as structured evidence context. Inject alongside source snippets in the synthesis prompt. Reuse the existing _build_diligence_context pattern from deep_research_service.py.

## Acceptance Criteria
Non-diligence research queries about energy topics receive relevant market data (prices, generation, demand, tariffs) in the synthesis prompt. The reasoning model cites specific quantitative data points from Zorora's own data stores. Market context injection is keyword-triggered, not manual.
