# SEP-067: Feedback as a ranking signal for research sources

**Status:** Open
**Created:** 2026-03-27
**Project:** zorora

## Summary
Use accumulated thumbs up/down feedback data to boost or demote sources in future deep research sessions

## Motivation
Thumbs up/down feedback on research chat responses is persisted to SQLite (SEP-059) but never reused. Sources that consistently appear in highly-rated responses should rank higher in future aggregation, and sources from poorly-rated responses should be demoted. This creates a knowledge compounding loop where user feedback improves research quality over time.

## Proposed Change
Compute a per-source feedback score from historical ratings across all research sessions. During source aggregation in aggregator.py, apply a feedback-based boost/penalty to source ranking alongside the existing credibility score. Sources with net-positive feedback get a boost; net-negative get a penalty. New/unrated sources are unaffected.

## Acceptance Criteria
Sources that appeared in thumbs-up-rated responses rank higher in subsequent research. Sources from thumbs-down responses rank lower. Feedback score is computed from research_feedback table joined through research_id to sources. Net-neutral and unrated sources are unaffected.
