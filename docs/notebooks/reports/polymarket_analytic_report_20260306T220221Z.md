What This Report Tests
This report analyzes the signal scores of options markets related to geopolitical and economic issues, focusing specifically on Iran-related events. It aims to distinguish between strong evidence of future outcomes and likely noise. The data is sourced from Polymarket options markets, which allows us to evaluate market predictions and identify potential signals of actual events.

The evaluation covers 35 options markets, with 2 showing high confidence and 31 showing likely noise. The report categorizes markets into two clusters: "Iran Strikes" and "Other."

Here's how the score works:
- The formula used to calculate the signal score is as follows: 0.50*max_6h_z + 0.30*(jump_24h_to_1h*100) + 0.20*(range_6h*100).
- A market can have a maximum score of 100, indicating a very high probability of the event occurring. However, any score over 50 should be considered with caution, as it suggests the likelihood of the event taking place, rather than a definitive outcome.

The data scope includes all options markets active on Polymarket during the specified period, which is from January 1, 2025, to December 31, 2026. The analysis focuses on the most recent available data at the time of the report's creation.

Key Findings
The report identifies the following key findings:
1. **High Confidence**: Two markets, with IDs 532742 and 532741, show high confidence levels. These are the markets where the signal score is above 50.
2. **Likely Noise**: The remaining 31 markets, with IDs ranging from 532762 to 539681, exhibit indications of noise. This does not mean they will definitely produce incorrect results but suggests they may not offer reliable predictive power.

These distinctions are made through a combination of the signal score, the market's cluster, and the nature of the underlying question being addressed.

What This Means
The presence of high confidence markets can suggest a reasonable expectation for the event predicted by those markets to occur within their specified window. However, the likelihood of this happening is not absolute, and caution is advised.

On the other hand, markets that show signs of noise do not necessarily indicate an absence of truth; rather, they should be interpreted cautiously, as there is no definitive way to determine whether they will produce correct or incorrect results.

What This Does Not Prove
It is important to emphasize that this report does not prove or disprove any criminal activity or provide definitive answers about the likelihood of events. The findings simply reflect the current state of market sentiment and do not guarantee the accuracy of the outcomes.
